About Me

Computational scientist working at the intersection of high-order numerical methods, kinetic theory, and machine learning for partial differential equations.

I am a computational scientist working at the Institute for Applied and Computational Mathematics, RWTH Aachen University, in the group of Prof. Manuel Torrilhon. My research is at the intersection of scientific computing, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and kinetic theory — designing high-order numerical methods that explain how shocks and instabilities propagate through complex flows.

I develop discontinuous Galerkin (DG) solvers for compressible multicomponent and rarefied gas flows, study Richtmyer–Meshkov and other hydrodynamic instabilities, and increasingly explore how machine learning and physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) can extract closures and accelerate simulations of strongly non-equilibrium flows.

Before Aachen, I was a Research Fellow at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore (2018–2022) and earned my PhD at Gyeongsang National University, South Korea (2018), with prior MTech training at IIT Madras. I have authored 65+ peer-reviewed papers, edited three books, and serve as Guest Editor and Editorial Board member for several journals.
