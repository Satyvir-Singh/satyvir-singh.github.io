Research Themes

Six interconnected research lines — from high-order numerical methods to machine-learning-augmented kinetic theory — applied to challenging compressible, multiphase and non-equilibrium flows.

🌊

Hydrodynamic Instabilities

Shock-driven Richtmyer–Meshkov and Rayleigh–Taylor instabilities in heavy/light bubbles, layered fluids, and tandem interfaces. High-fidelity DG simulations resolving vorticity generation, mixing layers, and Atwood/Mach number effects.

🧪

Rarefied & Non-Equilibrium Gas Flows

Boltzmann–Curtiss kinetic equation, Grad-13/14/17 moment closures, regularized 13-moment equations for rarefied polyatomic gases. Applications to hypersonic re-entry, micro-scale flows and aerospike geometries.

📐

High-Order Numerical Methods

Modal and mixed discontinuous Galerkin schemes, finite element solvers for compressible multicomponent flows, electrolyte models, and coupled reaction-diffusion systems on unstructured 2D/3D meshes.

🤖

Machine Learning for PDEs

Data-driven extraction of moment closures for strongly non-equilibrium flows, and physics-informed neural networks for solving CFD problems where classical kinetic closures break down.

⚡

Magnetohydrodynamics & Multiphase

Shock-MHD interaction with light/heavy bubbles, electron Boltzmann transport under electronic non-equilibrium, and multiphase flow morphology in compressible regimes.

🔥

Heat Transfer & Pattern Formation

Natural and mixed convection in eccentric annuli, thermal mixing and entropy generation, Turing pattern formation in Gray-Scott and FitzHugh-Nagumo reaction-diffusion systems.
